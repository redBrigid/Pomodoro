What is "Pomodoro"?

It's the technique which helps you to fight against procrastination (wandering and wasting time instead of doing things which you have to do). Seeing a hard task to do and concentrating on the result we feel discouraged and our brain wants to go from that unpleasant feeling and finds other things to do like browsing the internet or watching TV. But one of tricks is to concentrate on the process instead of on the result.

Process vs result

Concentrating on process means expecting the essay to be already written, home to be cleaned etc. what othen is overwhelming. But if our aim is to just sit for 25 minutes and do whatever, just the smallest step (but from our todo list!) is easier because we don't compare our current progress to expectations. So we extinct our sabotaging feelings and work becomes easier.

Breaks

It's good to make a 5 minutes break after each session. Move and stretch a bit, have some pleasure or reward for effort we just did like a piece of chocolate, playing a game etc. It's important to rewire our brains which uses dopamine to make us linking procrastination and disliking things to do. Having a reward after the pomodoro sessions we cheat our brain that such session is something what we like and it makes things easier as we don't need to fight against ourselves.

Why name "Pomodoro"?

Because of the kitchen tool which counts time for boiling eggs, baking cake etc. These tools can have fancy shapes, including tomato (pomodoro) which was the inspiration to make this technique.

Have fun!

There is no method which will be perfect for everyone. Feel free to try it out, adjust or whatever. Remember - the method is for you, not you for method.
